Blair Simmons - Manager, Compliance at Maritime NZ¹ See numbers in red below (Figure 1) for a typical Panamax container ship that can carry up to 18,000 twenty-foot containers.

These ships have a fuel capacity of around 16,000 metric tons and currently a metric ton of heavy fuel oil costs $833, so about $13.3 Million to fuel up!!

The shipping line charges around $7,500 per container to get from anywhere in the World to NZ. This means for one full shipload of containers the total fees charged to the customers would be over $135 Million, so the fuel cost is only around 10% of the operating cost to run the ship.

For comparison though, keep in mind that the value of all the goods in 18,000 containers on this size ship could easily be over $1 Billion once sold in NZ.

This type of ship uses around 230 metric tons of heavy fuel per day at 20 knots, so does about 480 nautical miles / 890 kms per day and costs $192,000 per day in fuel at this speed. On this basis, their fuel tanks should last for 70 days or around 60,000kms range on (in perfect weather).
